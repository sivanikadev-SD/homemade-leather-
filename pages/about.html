Our Story

The Founding

Born in a workshop in Trichy

In 1987, master artisan Krishnamurthy Rajan set up a small workshop with a single sewing awl, a cutting board, and a roll of waxed thread. He had spent a decade learning the craft from an Italian saddle-maker in Pondicherry and returned home with one vision: to make leather goods worthy of the world's finest ateliers, right here in Tamil Nadu.

Today the atelier is led by his granddaughter Kavitha — but the tools, the methods, and the philosophy remain unchanged. Every awl hole is punched by hand. Every edge burnished by thumb.

"We do not make things quickly. We make them properly."
— Krishnamurthy Rajan, Founder

What We Believe

The three pillars of our craft

I

Slow Craft

We reject the culture of speed. A well-made piece takes the time it takes. We would rather disappoint a deadline than compromise a stitch.

II

Honest Materials

We use only full-grain leather. Never bonded, never split-grain, never corrected. If it has a scar, we leave it — it is part of the hide's story.

III

Lifetime Ownership

When you buy from us, you buy forever. We repair every Cordovan piece that walks through our door — no questions, no fees, no expiry.

Our Journey

38 years of unbroken craft

1987

The Workshop Opens

Krishnamurthy returns from Pondicherry and opens a 200 sq ft workshop with a single bench, two tools, and infinite ambition.

1999

First Corporate Commission

A Bangalore bank orders 50 personalised leather diaries. Word spreads fast. The atelier doubles in size and takes on its first apprentices.

2011

Kavitha Takes the Helm

After four years training in Paris, Kavitha returns to lead the atelier. She introduces new leather types and the bespoke commission programme.

2026

Today

Cordovan Atelier ships to 12+ countries, maintains a waitlist for bespoke pieces, and still operates from the same city where it all began.
